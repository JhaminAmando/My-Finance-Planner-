💰 My Finance Planner — 
Free Excel TemplateA free, practical Excel finance planner built to help you track income, manage expenses, and hit your savings goals — week by week and month by month. Designed with the Ghanaian cedi (₵) in mind, but works for anyone.
📋 What's Inside
🗂️ Sheet 1 — Dashboard
A live summary that pulls data automatically from your Monthly Planner. At a glance you can see:

Total income vs. budgeted income
Essential and lifestyle expenses vs. budget
Net balance and savings rate
Your savings goal tracker with a months-to-goal countdown

📅 Sheet 2 — Monthly Planner
Plan and track your full month in one place:

Income sources: salary, freelance, side hustle, other
Essential expenses: food, transport, utilities, phone, health
Lifestyle expenses: eating out, clothing, subscriptions, personal care
Savings and investments: goals, stocks, business fund
Auto-calculated net balance and savings rate with status indicators

📆 Sheet 3 — Weekly Planner
Track spending day by day across a full week:

Log daily income and expenses by category (Mon–Sun)
Auto-totalling weekly net balance
Reflection prompt at the bottom to review and improve

📖 Sheet 4 — How To Use
Step-by-step instructions for getting started, with a colour key explaining which cells to edit and which are auto-calculated.

🚀 How to Download & Use

Click on the .xlsx file in this repository
Click the "Download raw file" button (top right of the file preview)
Open it in Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets
Enter your own income and expense figures in the blue cells
All totals, differences, and dashboards update automatically


🎨 Colour Key
ColourMeaning🔵 Blue cellsEnter your data here (inputs)⚫ Black/auto cellsAuto-calculated — do not edit🟢 Green statusOn track / within budget🔴 Red/Orange statusOver budget / needs attention

💡 Tips for Getting the Most Out of It

Update your Weekly Planner every Sunday evening — small consistent habits matter
Fill in the Monthly Planner on the 1st of each month with your budgeted amounts
Aim for 20%+ of your income going to savings each month
Keep essential expenses below 60% of your income
Reduce lifestyle spending first when you need to free up money
